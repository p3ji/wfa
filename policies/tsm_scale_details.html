Transition Support Measure (TSM) Scale Table

The Transition Support Measure (TSM) is a cash payment based on an employee's years of service in the public service, paid in weeks of salary. For indeterminate seasonal and part-time employees, the TSM is pro-rated in the same manner as severance pay under the terms of the applicable collective agreement. Severance pay provisions found in collective agreements are in addition to the TSM.

Work Force Adjustment Options for Opting Employees

Indeterminate employees whose positions are affected by a workforce adjustment and who are declared opting employees have 120 days to choose one of three options:

Option A: 12-Month Surplus Priority Period

Under Option A, the employee remains a surplus employee for a 12-month surplus priority period to find another position in the public service. During this time, they have priority for appointment to other positions for which they qualify. If they do not find alternative employment within the 12 months, they are laid off.

Option B: Transition Support Measure (TSM)

Under Option B, the employee resigns immediately or on a date agreed upon, and receives a lump-sum cash payout called the Transition Support Measure (TSM) based on their years of service, as set out in the TSM scale. Selecting Option B means the employee relinquishes all priority rights for reappointment.

Option C: TSM and Education Allowance

Under Option C, the employee receives the Transition Support Measure (TSM) payout plus an Education Allowance of up to $17,000 for tuition, books, and mandatory equipment for education or retraining. Option C is split into two options: Option C(i) where the employee resigns immediately and receives the allowance, and Option C(ii) where the employee takes a leave without pay for up to two years to pursue education, after which they resign or are laid off.

Pension Reduction Waiver for Opting Employees

A pension reduction waiver allows eligible employees who retire involuntarily due to a WFA situation to receive an unreduced pension, waivering the standard age-based reductions.

Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for a pension reduction waiver under workforce adjustment, the employee must meet specific criteria:

  • The employee must retire involuntarily, either through resignation under Option B (TSM) or being laid off.
  • The pension reduction waiver is generally not applicable if the employee selects Option C (Education Allowance).
  • The age and service requirements depend on when the employee joined the Public Service Superannuation Plan:
  • For Group 1 members (joined before January 1, 2013): The employee must be at least 55 years of age with at least 10 years of pensionable service at the end of their employment. There is no waiver for employees aged 50-54.
  • For Group 2 members (joined on or after January 1, 2013): The employee must be at least 60 years of age with at least 10 years of pensionable service at the end of their employment. There is no waiver for employees aged 55-59 in Group 2.
